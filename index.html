Gut Training

Your gut is a muscle. At 11 hours of running, you need 200–300 calories per hour or you WILL hit the wall. Your body stores only ~90 minutes of glycogen. Everything after that is nutrition-dependent. Your gut needs weeks to learn to absorb calories while running — start now.

Phase 1 · Jun
Foundation
Runs under 20km: Water only, sip every 15 min.

Runs 20–30km: 1 gel at km 10, 1 at km 20. Practice drinking while running — don't stop.
Phase 2 · Jul Wk 1–3
Building
Runs 25–35km: 2 gels + sports drink. Gel every 45–50 min from km 10.

Try 2 different gel brands. Pick your favorite by end of July.
Phase 3 · Jul B2B + Aug
Full Protocol
Runs 35–60km: Gel every 40 min + real food. Rice balls, dates, banana, PB&J. Add salt tabs every 60 min from km 30.
Phase 4 · Sep
Race Rehearsal
Taper long runs: Exact race nutrition. Same gels, same brand, same timing. Confirm your full 100km fueling plan on Sep 6 long run.

Gut Training Rules

✅ Do This

  • Always eat your first gel with water (not sports drink)
  • Test multiple gel brands in training: GU, SIS, Maurten
  • Practice eating real food at pace: rice balls, dates, banana
  • Introduce caffeine gels in training from late July, only from km 50+
  • Train with the SAME gels you'll use on race day
  • Add salt to all long run food — you're losing sodium in sweat
  • If nauseous: slow to walk, eat a date, drink water, keep moving

❌ Never Do This

  • Skip nutrition on runs over 20km — ever
  • Use a new gel brand on race day for the first time
  • Take a gel without water (causes stomach cramps)
  • Drink only plain water for 11+ hours (hyponatremia risk)
  • Go more than 50 min without calories during race
  • Eat a big heavy meal within 2 hrs of running
  • Ignore gut pain — slow down, eat, keep moving

Hydration

Daily Hydration

  • 500ml water immediately on waking
  • 3–4L total on non-run days
  • 4–5L on long run days
  • Add electrolytes to 1L per day (sodium + potassium)
  • Urine check: pale yellow = hydrated. Dark = drink more now.
  • No alcohol (you're 15 anyway — good)

During Long Runs

  • Carry minimum 1.5L in hydration vest/belt
  • Sip 150–200ml every 20 min
  • Never drink only plain water for 11hrs — hyponatremia risk
  • Alternate: water → sports drink → water → sports drink
  • If cramping mid-run: salt tab immediately + slow to walk

3-Day Strength Plan

Three sessions per week, scheduled on easy run days. Focus: injury prevention, glute activation, hip stability, and core control. No heavy lifting — this is running support work, not bodybuilding. Each session takes 25–35 minutes.

Schedule: Do Strength A on Tuesday, Strength B on Thursday, Strength C on Saturday (after your long run only in June — in July/August, do it 2 hrs before or as a separate session). In taper month (Sep), drop to 2 sessions/week.

Session A — Glute & Hip Stability (Tue)

01
Glute Bridge
3 × 20 reps · 60 sec rest
Lie on back, feet flat, drive hips up. Squeeze glutes at top for 2 sec. This directly fixes anterior pelvic tilt and activates your running engine.
02
Single-Leg Glute Bridge
3 × 12 each leg · 60 sec rest
Same as above but one leg extended. Identifies and corrects left-right imbalances. Critical for preventing knee issues on long runs.
03
Clamshell
3 × 15 each side · 45 sec rest
Side-lying, knees bent at 45°, feet together. Open top knee like a clamshell. Works hip abductors — prevents ITB syndrome and knee pain.
04
Dead Bug
3 × 10 each side · 60 sec rest
On back, arms up, knees at 90°. Extend opposite arm/leg while keeping lower back pressed to floor. Core stability for efficient running form.
05
Lateral Band Walk
3 × 15 steps each way
Mini band above knees, half-squat position. Sidestep 15 steps each direction. Activates glute med — protects knees on downhill sections.
06
Calf Raises
3 × 20 · both legs then single
On edge of step. Full range — heel drop to toe raise. Prevents shin splints and Achilles issues. Essential at high weekly mileage.

Session B — Core & Posterior Chain (Thu)

01
Plank
3 × 45 sec · progress to 60 sec by Aug
Forearms down, body straight. Don't let hips sag or rise. Builds the anti-rotation core stability runners need at km 60+.
02
Side Plank
3 × 30 sec each side
Elbow under shoulder, body straight. Hip must stay up. Lateral stability prevents hip drop mid-stride which causes knee and IT band issues.
03
Romanian Deadlift
3 × 12 reps · bodyweight or light DB
Hinge at hips, slight knee bend, feel hamstrings stretch. Trains the posterior chain you use for every uphill stride. Go light and slow.
04
Reverse Lunge
3 × 10 each leg · bodyweight
Step backward, knee nearly touching floor. More hip dominant than forward lunge — mimics trail running mechanics and protects the knee.
05
Hip Thrust (Floor)
3 × 15 · back on bench or floor
Upper back on bench, feet flat, barbell or bodyweight on hips. Drive up, glutes at top. The #1 exercise for fixing anterior pelvic tilt.
06
Bird Dog
3 × 10 each side · slow and controlled
On all fours, extend opposite arm and leg. Hold 3 sec at top. Spinal stability and glute activation combined. Perfect APT correction exercise.

Session C — Ankle, Balance & Running Specifics (Sat or 2nd Tue)

01
Single-Leg Balance
3 × 45 sec each leg
Eyes open, then progress to eyes closed. Ankle stability prevents sprains on uneven trail surfaces. Essential for 100km race terrain.
02

20-Min Daily Cooldown

Do this after every run. Focused on: touching toes, hip flexor release, hamstring lengthening, fixing anterior pelvic tilt (APT), and activating glutes. Each session flows in order — don't skip ahead. Hold each stretch fully — rushing defeats the purpose.

⚠️ APT Warning Signs

  • Lower back pain during or after runs → APT likely culprit
  • Knee pain on downhills → weak glutes + tight hip flexors
  • "Duck feet" (toes pointing out when running) → hip rotation issue
  • Tightness always in the same hip flexor → check leg stride symmetry
  • If lower back pain persists more than 3 days → see a physio immediately
